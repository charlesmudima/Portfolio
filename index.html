GIT713 Overview

GIT713 provides an introduction to professionalism, ethics, legislation, strategy, and implementation in the geographic information system industry. These principles are taught through a project implementation and class presentations. Additionally, the module offers an understanding of data management and quality. In my experience with GIT713, I have found that the module aims to provide a practical understanding of professional practice in the GIS industry. It covers a range of topics, including GIS Strategy and Implementation, Professionalism and Ethics, Business Communication, Project and Software Development, GIS Data Management, and a Botanical Garden group project. It is important to note that the GIS industry is composed of various components. The mind map below illustrates some of the important and helpful components I have encountered in this course. The mind map illustrates core components that have been fundamental and helpful in my GIT713 journey. These principles will assist me in my future career as a GIS professional. It's worth noting that the components shown on the mind map can overlap or be related to each other. For instance, human resources are essential in the GIS industry, and effective communication is necessary to maximize this resource. This aspect was crucial to the progression of our botanical project.

Botanical Garden Project

For this project, we collaborated with Dr. Kirkwood the botanical gardenâ€™s curator to develop a prototype map. As a group, we had to understand the client's requirements and implement the principles of project management, GIS strategy, and software development.This project allowed us to apply the theoretical principles we learned in class and utilize our skills and knowledge. Initially, it was challenging to understand the client's needs, but frequent consultations with our client and supervisors helped us overcome this challenge. In the first phase, we developed a conceptual design based on the client's requirements and the available resources and tools. We identified the following software and tools to aid us in solving the client's problem: ArcGIS Pro, Leaflet, ArcGIS Online, Excel, QGIS, and Visual Studio Code. To plan our project, we divided our map functionalities into core, secondary, and tertiary functionalities. This approach helped us understand and strategize our project solution. We also utilized a Gantt chart to complete our project and interim deliverables. This tool helped us focus on the different phases of the project. After completing the conceptual design, we focused on the logical design phase. During this phase, we constructed a database prototype to demonstrate how the data would be stored and how the different attributes of the botanical garden would be linked. This phase allowed me to appreciate the concept of database management, as I learned how to organize and structure data effectively. In the later stages of the project, we focused on finalizing our product and ensuring that we met the goals we had set for ourselves. Project Management

Project management is another key component that I identified as important in my GIT713 course. Project management is defined as a temporary process that requires a set of tangible and intangible resources to achieve a specific set of objectives or goals. Working on the botanical project made me value project management as a practice and skill. Implementing the theory of project management into practice was not easy, but it was a valuable learning experience. Project management consists of various components, namely organization, objectives, project team, and goals. In our botanical project, we tried to define and accommodate all the components that define project management. We also implemented the five different steps in project management: 1) Initiation, 2) Planning, 3) Execution, 4) Monitoring and Control, and 5) Closure. One important aspect I learned in project management was that a team can make or break a project. The team is at the center of any project, and their capabilities and abilities greatly influence the outcome of the project. Another important aspect of project management is the project leader. In our botanical group, we did not have a leader, as we all knew our roles and responsibilities. However, in most projects, a project manager is necessary to allocate competent individuals to specific tasks, hold people accountable, and ensure that the team is working as efficiently as possible.

GIS Strategy and Implementation

Over the past decade, the GIS industry has experienced significant growth due to advances in technology and greater access to data, among other factors. This growth has brought principles such as GIS strategy and implementation to the forefront, with the aim of successfully integrating GIS practices into organizations. To achieve this, GIS strategy and implementation requires a vision, strategy, and roadmap. A Geographic Information System (GIS) is an integrated collection of computer software and data used to view and manage information about geographic places, analyze spatial relationships, and model spatial processes. GIS provides a framework for gathering and organizing spatial data and related information, enabling it to be displayed and analyzed. Strategic planning for the management of geographic information is an ongoing process that requires a comprehensive, long-term perspective to provide focus and direction for more detailed tactical planning over time.

Software Development

Software development is the practice of utilising various programming languages to create software. Software development is a very broad field and can be broken down into three main categories: web development, mobile development and desktop development. During the GIT713 course, I had the opportunity to work with various software programs. One of the data wrangling tasks involved creating a voter's turnout map for the Western Cape province using ArcPy and R-Studio, which allowed me to expand and improve my coding skills in Python. I found this task interesting and relatable. Additionally, the lecture on software product development by Juan was another valuable aspect of the course. As someone with a background in computer science, it solidified my understanding and knowledge of software development. I was pleased to discover that the same principles I learned in my CS344 module can be applied in the GIS industry. Software development often involves different people working together, making it a team effort that requires effective communication and coordination. This component also highlights the importance of human resources. Through software development, I learned about the need for clear and concise communication, which aligns with the 7 C's of communication (Completeness, Concreteness, Courtesy, Correctness, Clarity, Consideration, Conciseness). I also gained knowledge of SCRUM as an agile project management technique.

Professionalism and Ethics

Although I found the professionalism and ethics section to be the least enjoyable part of the course due to the extensive literature and theory we covered, I recognized its significance in the GIS industry. This module taught me the importance of conducting oneself professionally and adhering to ethical principles in the workplace. Moreover, it reinforced the notion that knowledge is power, particularly when it comes to laws and regulations governing data. The professionalism and ethics section covered several topics, including the South African Geomatics Council (SAGC), the seven processes for ethical decision-making, and moral case studies. As we embark on our professional journeys, these topics proved to be very enlightening and crucial. The seven-step process for ethical decision-making, in particular, stood out to me as a valuable takeaway from this section. The process involves: Step 1: State the problem Step 2: Check the facts Step 3: Identify relevant factors Step 4: Develop a list of options Step 5: Test the options Step 6: Make a choice based on Steps 1-5 Step 7: Review Steps 1-6
